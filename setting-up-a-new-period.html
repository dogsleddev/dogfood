Setup · User Guides · Updating actuals

Setting up a new period

Setting up a new period is the monthly close roll-forward: you bring in the just-closed month's actuals from your ERP and systems of record, advance Dogfood's one global as-of date so that month flips from in close to Actual, and let Base reforecast the rest of the year while the locked Budget snapshot stays put, all on trial-balance-driven statements that the sub-ledger reconciles up to.

What setting up a new period is

Setting up a new period is the monthly close roll-forward. It is how you move Dogfood forward in time after your books close, and it is the same calm, ordered ritual every month:

  • Your ERP closes the month.
  • You import it into Dogfood.
  • You validate and reconcile the detail up to the trial balance.
  • You advance the global as-of date, and the in-close month flips to Actual while Base reforecasts.

Dogfood does not run the close. It reads a clean close from your ERP. That is the plan-to-perform side of the line: your ERP and your accounting team own record-to-report (posting entries, reconciling subledgers, finalizing the books), and Dogfood reads the finished result to plan the future off it. Dogfood never posts a journal entry, never reconciles a bank account, and never re-summes your books.

So "setting up a new period" means two things happening together:

  • You bring in the just-closed month's actuals (the trial balance plus the supporting detail).
  • You advance Dogfood's clock so that month becomes Actual and the forecast rolls forward off the new reality.

Everything in this guide is built so that nothing silently changes a statement. The trial balance is authoritative, the statements are a view of it, and every check below tells you exactly what it guarantees.

The global as-of date and the three month states

Dogfood has one app-wide close boundary, a single global as-of date. It is not a per-report setting. Every surface (the Dashboard, the statements, the registers, the drivers, Scout) reads the same boundary, so the whole app always agrees on what is closed and what is still a forecast.

Each month sits in exactly one of three states:

  • Actual (closed): the books are final. The numbers come straight from the imported trial balance.
  • In close: the boundary month, provisional. The month your team is finishing now. Numbers may still move until you import the final close.
  • Forecast: future months, generated by the layer-2 drivers (Revenue, Cost of Revenue, Personnel, Expense, AR, and the balance-sheet drivers).

For Bearing today:

  • Actual: January 2024 through May 2026.
  • In close: June 2026.
  • Forecast: July 2026 through December 2026.

The company chip in the rail reads FY26 · Jun, which is the as-of month. When you import a new month, the as-of advances and these three bands all shift together by one month.

Statements are trial-balance-driven

This is the core guarantee, so it is worth being precise about it.

Your ERP's trial balance is authoritative. It is already summed and already adjusted (it includes every manual entry, accrual, and reclass your team booked during the close). Dogfood's three statements (Forecasted P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow) are a view of the trial balance, assembled through the chart of accounts. They are never re-summed from a line-item feed.

The transaction sub-ledger (the expense_transactions detail and any other document-level feeds) rides alongside the statements. It exists for two jobs: drilling into a number to see what makes it up, and Flux Analysis. It does not build the statement. Instead it reconciles up to the trial balance:

Σ (sub-ledger detail for an account in a month)  ===  that account's trial balance movement

So drill-to-transaction is for explanation, not derivation. The statement number is the trial balance number. The detail's job is to explain it, and reconciliation (below) tells you whether that explanation is complete.

What you import for a new period

You import in Setup > Data Import using the CSV/XLSX templates. Every template upserts on a stable id: re-importing a period overwrites the existing rows for that period, it never appends duplicates and never asks you to re-key anything.

From the ERP (this drives the numbers):

  • trial_balance.csv every month, the financials. One row per (period, account_code). Balance-sheet accounts carry the period-end balance; P&L accounts carry the fiscal-year-to-date activity; exactly one of debit / credit is non-zero per row.
  • chart_of_accounts.csv imported at setup and whenever the COA changes. Its statement_line column is Account Mapping: it is the map from each account_code to exactly one statement line.

The sub-ledger detail (for drill and flux):

  • expense_transactions.csv, AP and vendor bills, one row per document, each with a stable transaction_id.
  • Optional journal_entries.csv for GL-level drill. Payroll, billing, and receipts detail follow the same row-per-document shape.

From each system of record:

  • headcount.csv from your HRIS, feeds Staff and Personnel.
  • customers.csv from Billing/CRM, feeds Customers and ARR.
  • vendors.csv from AP, the vendor names.
  • ar_aging.csv / ap_aging.csv from the ERP aging.
  • budget.csv once, at plan lock.
  • fx_rates.csv, a single identity row (Bearing is USD-only).

What you do NOT import: the forward-looking, Dogfood-native data. Your forecasts, your scenarios, your planned hires, and Account Mapping itself all live in Dogfood. Importing actuals never overwrites them. Importing a new month reforecasts the remaining periods off the new actuals, but the assumptions you set stay yours.

Validation: what each gate guarantees

When you import, Dogfood runs five checks before anything lands. Each one guarantees a specific property of a clean period.

  • The trial balance balances. Sum of debit equals sum of credit for the period. This is the hard gate: a TB that does not foot is not a close, and the import stops here. (Bearing's real May 2026 trial_balance foots to $59,567,335.23 on each side.)
  • Every account is known. Every account_code in the trial balance exists in chart_of_accounts. No mystery accounts.
  • Every account is mapped. Every account maps to exactly one statement_line: no orphans, no gaps. This is what guarantees every dollar in the TB has a home on a statement.
  • One row per (period, account_code). A trial balance has a single row per account per period, so re-importing a period cleanly overwrites it instead of double-counting.
  • Period format and continuity. period is YYYY-MM, and there are no gaps across the closed range.

Reconciliation: detail vs trial balance

Validation proves the statements are clean. Reconciliation proves the explanation underneath them is complete. The two are separate on purpose.

The statements always equal the trial balance, because the TB is the source and it balances. So reconciliation never checks the statement against the detail to decide the statement. It checks the detail against the TB, per (account, period):

  • Tie (gap within the materiality threshold): the account is reconciled. The sub-ledger fully explains the trial balance movement.
  • Variance (gap above the threshold): the account is flagged with the dollar gap. You see a per-account reconciliation status rolled up onto each statement line, an exception list, and on drill the explicit breakdown: "TB $X, detail $Y, unreconciled $Z."

A materiality threshold (for example $1, or 0.1% of the line) keeps rounding noise off the exception list so you only see real gaps.

A gap means the line-item feed is missing or mis-tagging something the ERP booked: a manual accrual, a reclass, a journal entry with no sub-ledger document behind it. The statement number does not move while you sort it out. There are no plugs: everything must reconcile. A gap is never force-balanced with an "uncategorized" reconciling line, and the trial balance is never bent to the detail. There is one honest path:

  • Fix upstream. Pull the missing journal entry or accrual into the feed, or correct the mapping in Account Mapping, then re-import. The gap clears on its own.

Until it clears, the account stays on the exception list as a blocking "unreconciled / needs attention" flag with its dollar gap, so you can see exactly what is unexplained and how much. The statement is unchanged the whole time. Reconciliation is purely about whether the explanation is complete, never about editing the answer.

Advancing the as-of

Importing the new month is what advances the global as-of date. When the import for month N lands and clears its checks:

  • The in-close month flips to Actual.
  • The next month becomes the new in-close month.
  • The Forecast horizon shrinks by one month.
  • Base reforecasts the remaining months off the new actuals. Base is the working forecast and the app default, so it keeps moving as reality comes in.
  • Budget / Variance now compares actual month N against the locked plan.

All surfaces move together, because there is one boundary. And the one thing that does not move is the locked Budget snapshot. Budget is a frozen snapshot of the layer-2 drivers, taken at plan lock with lockBudget and immutable after. That is the whole point of Budget: it is the yardstick. Base chases reality; Budget holds still so variance means something.

The override layer

Sometimes you need to correct a synced record by hand (a misclassified vendor, a known-stale headcount row) before the upstream system catches up. Dogfood handles this with an override layer.

An override is a delta off the synced base, the same pattern as scenario_inputs (scenario adjustments off Base) and flux_notes (review notes on a transaction). It is flagged so it is always visible as a manual edit, and it persists across re-imports. The imported source data stays immutable underneath. Your edit layers on top, so re-importing the period does not wipe your correction, and removing the override returns the record to the synced value.

Restatement, re-import, and reopen

Three things you will do after a month is closed, and what each one touches.

  • Re-import a prior month (restatement). When your ERP restates a closed month, drop in the corrected trial_balance (and any corrected detail). Because the import upserts on the (period, account_code) key, this is an idempotent overwrite: the old rows for that period are replaced. Dogfood re-rolls the GL and forecast forward and recomputes Variance, but it does not move the as-of, because you are correcting history, not advancing time.
  • Reopen a month. Move the global as-of back in Settings. Later months reclassify from Actual back to Forecast until you re-advance. No re-import is needed to reopen.
  • Durable work survives both. Flux notes key on the stable transaction_id and snapshot the amount_at_note (the figure as it stood when the note was written). So if a restatement moves a number, the note card flags it (a flux on the flux) instead of silently going stale. If a transaction disappears in a restatement, its note is flagged orphaned and needs re-review, never quietly lost.

The connectors roadmap

Today the mechanism is CSV/XLSX batch import: you export from each system and drop the files into Setup > Data Import.

A live connector (Salesforce, Rippling, the ERP API) will later replace the CSV step behind the same DataStore seam, so nothing about the model above changes. The key idea: the CSV template is the contract a connector fills. A connector maps its source objects into the same columns the template defines, and upserts on the same stable id. (See connectors/salesforce.md for the worked example: Salesforce Account / Opportunity / Contract map into customers / pipeline / contracts, upserting on the Salesforce Id. Salesforce owns the sales domain only; recognized revenue, AR, and cash still come from billing and the ERP.)

So when connectors arrive, your monthly routine gets shorter, not different.

Worked example: closing June 2026

Bearing's as-of is FY26 · Jun, so June 2026 is in close. Here is the full roll-forward.

Closing June 2026, step by step

  1. Confirm the close. Your accounting team finalizes June in the ERP: every entry posted, subledgers reconciled, the books locked.
  2. Export and import. Export the June trial balance to trial_balance.csv and the period's AP detail to expense_transactions.csv, refresh any domain feeds that changed (customers.csv, headcount.csv, vendors.csv, the aging files), and drop them all into Setup > Data Import.
  3. Validate. The importer checks the five gates: the TB foots (debits equal credits), every account_code is known, every account maps to one statement_line, one row per (period, account_code), period is YYYY-MM with no gaps. The footing check is the hard gate.
  4. Reconcile. For each account, the sub-ledger detail is summed and compared to the TB movement. Accounts that tie are marked reconciled. Any gap above the threshold is flagged with its dollar amount on the exception list as a blocking "unreconciled / needs attention" item. There are no plugs: you clear each one by fixing upstream and re-importing (pull the missing accrual, or correct the mapping). The statement numbers do not move while you work the list.
  5. Advance. With June clean, the as-of advances: June 2026 flips from in close to Actual, July 2026 becomes the new in-close month, the forecast horizon becomes August through December 2026, Base reforecasts Jul-Dec off the new June actuals, and the Budget snapshot stays frozen so June's actual-vs-budget variance reads true.

The chip now reads FY26 · Jul, and every surface in the app agrees.

Monthly close-import SOP checklist

Run this every month, in order:

  • 1. Confirm the ERP close is final for the month (books locked, nothing pending). Dogfood reads a clean close; do not import a month still open in the ERP.
  • 2. Export the trial balance to trial_balance.csv.
  • 3. Export the period's sub-ledger detail (expense_transactions.csv, plus any other document feeds you load).
  • 4. Refresh the domain feeds that changed (customers.csv, headcount.csv, vendors.csv, ar_aging.csv / ap_aging.csv).
  • 5. Import the files in Setup > Data Import.
  • 6. Clear validation. The TB must balance (the hard gate); fix and re-export if it does not. Confirm known accounts, complete mapping, one row per (period, account_code), and YYYY-MM periods.
  • 7. Work the reconciliation exception list. For each flagged account, fix upstream and re-import (no plugs). Confirm the statement number never moved.
  • 8. Confirm the as-of advanced and that Base reforecast the remaining months.
  • 9. Review Budget vs Actual variance for the newly closed month against the frozen Budget snapshot.

Asking Scout about a new period

Scout reads the same spine these surfaces do, so it can answer questions about where you are in the cycle and what an import did. Useful asks:

  • "What is the current as-of date, and which months are Actual versus Forecast?"
  • "Which accounts are flagged in reconciliation for the latest period, and by how much?" Scout reads the per-account reconciliation status and reports the flagged gaps with their dollar amounts.
  • "How does June actual compare to budget now that it closed?"
  • "What happens to Budget when I advance the period?" Scout will explain that Base reforecasts while the locked Budget snapshot stays frozen.

Scout never re-derives the books. It reads the trial-balance-driven statements and the reconciliation status, the same clean close you imported, and explains what it sees. That is Dogfood's whole posture in one line: your ERP closes the books, and Dogfood plans your next move off them.
